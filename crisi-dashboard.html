CRISI Project — State Rail Safety Research & Planning
State Rail Safety Research & Planning
A CRISI project from the University of Delaware and RailState
About the Project
Actual measured volumes of the hazardous materials moving by rail.
The CRISI Hazmat project, led by the University of Delaware and RailState, LLC, is deploying a network of trackside sensors across six participating states to measure every train that passes — including the specific UN placard on every tank car. The result is a direct, independent count of the hazardous materials actually moving by rail — what is moving, in what volumes, along which corridors, and in which direction — at a level of detail communities have never previously had.
Each participating State DOT and emergency management agency works directly with the University of Delaware to put this data to use — coordinating with first responders, overlaying hazmat flow against population density and environmentally sensitive areas, and grounding safety and infrastructure planning in observed data. Participants also gain access to all RailState data for their state, extending its value beyond hazmat. Data access, custom analysis, training, and ongoing support continue throughout the three-year grant period.
Project Goals
  • ›Establish independent, directly measured volumes of the hazardous materials actually moving by rail across participating states
  • ›Replace limited, lagging data with car-level, UN-placard-level detail for emergency planning and risk assessment
  • ›Give first responders and state agencies an accurate picture of exactly what moves through their jurisdictions
  • ›Overlay hazmat flow against population density and environmentally sensitive areas to surface the highest-risk corridors
  • ›Ground state rail-safety and infrastructure planning in observed movements rather than estimates
What We Deliver
  • ›Trackside sensor coverage of the key rail corridors in each participating state
  • ›A custom analysis dashboard for each state, tailored to its corridors and commodities
  • ›A mapping tool overlaying hazmat flow against population and sensitive areas, plus a preliminary findings report
Benefits to States & Responders
  • ›An actionable, directly measured picture of the hazardous materials actually moving through their jurisdictions
  • ›Objective data measured from photographic evidence, independent of railroad reporting
  • ›Full access to all RailState data covering each participant’s state
  • ›An evidence base for emergency planning, training, and infrastructure prioritization
Project Steps
Each participating state follows the same five-stage workflow over the three-year grant.
STEP 01
Sensor Deployment
RailState installs trackside sensors along the key rail corridors in each participating state.
STEP 02
Direct Volume Measurement
Every train that passes is measured at car-level detail — including the UN placard on every tank car — producing actual counts of what moved, not estimates.
STEP 03
Custom Dashboard
A state-specific dashboard is built around the corridors, commodities, and communities unique to each participant.
STEP 04
Mapping & Findings
Hazmat flows are overlaid against population density and sensitive areas, with a report identifying risk corridors and planning priorities.
STEP 05
Training & Support
State DOT and emergency-management analysts receive training, full data access, and ongoing support throughout the grant.
What Participating States Can Do With the Data
The dashboards and underlying data support a range of safety, emergency-planning, and infrastructure applications.
Strengthen Emergency Response
UN-placard-level hazmat data lets first responders and local emergency planning committees know what is actually moving through their jurisdictions and prepare accordingly.
Assess Community Risk
Overlay hazmat flow against population density and environmentally sensitive areas to identify and prioritize the highest-risk corridors and crossings.
Inform State Rail Planning
Volume, commodity mix, train type, and corridor data feed directly into state rail plans and long-range safety investment decisions.
Prioritize Crossing & Infrastructure Safety
Train length and frequency estimates support grade-crossing blockage analysis and the prioritization of separation and safety projects.
Engage Railroads with Independent Data
Work from objective, measured movements rather than railroad-reported or modeled figures when coordinating on safety and routing.
Support Incident Review & Drills
A measured record of what moved, when, and where provides an evidence base for after-action review, preparedness exercises, and risk modeling.

About RailState
The largest independent rail sensor network in North America.
RailState operates the largest independent rail sensor network in North America. Our trackside sensors photograph every train that passes, capturing car-level detail — equipment type, commodity markings, hazmat placards, container ownership, railroad, and direction of travel — across hundreds of strategic locations.
Unlike government statistics, industry surveys, or modeled estimates, RailState data is directly measured from photographic evidence. Every data point in this dashboard traces back to a physical observation of a real rail car at a known location and time. We are not an aggregator, we do not model or estimate volumes, and we are not affiliated with any railroad, shipper, or trading firm. This independence is what makes RailState data uniquely valuable for benchmarking, validation, and forward-looking intelligence.

RailState deploys proprietary trackside sensor stations across the North American rail network. From the moment a train passes a sensor to the moment structured data reaches our customers — the entire pipeline completes in under 30 minutes.
